Orange creamily Sheet Cake



Ingredients
  • 1 (15.25 ounce) white or yellow cake mix
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup orange juice
  • 1/3 cup oil
  • 1 orange, zested
  • 1 (6 ounce) box instant orange jello
  • 2 cups boiling water
  • 1 (3.4 ounce) box vanilla pudding
    • 1 cup milk
  • (8 ounce) tub frozen whipped topping, such as Cool Whip®, thawed
 Directions
  1. Gather all ingredients. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (180 degrees C). Spray a 9x13-inch pan with nonstick cooking spray. Set aside.
  2. Combine cake mix, eggs, orange juice, oil, and orange zest in a bowl and mix until just combined. Pour into the prepared pan.
  3. Bake in the preheated oven until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean, 28 to 35 minutes. Cool for 15 minutes.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean, 28 to 35 minutes. Cool for 15 minutes.
  5. Using the handle of a wooden spoon, poke holes throughout cake.
  6. Add orange jello to boiling water and stir until dissolved. Pour jello mixture over cake. Refrigerate until cool, 2 to 3 hours.
  7. Whisk vanilla pudding mix and milk together until thickened, about 2 minutes. Fold in Cool Whip.
  8. Spread mixture over cooled cake and refrigerate overnight.

Orange Cream Fudge



Ingredients
  • 3 cups white sugar
  • ¾ cup butter
  • 1 (7 ounce) jar marshmallow creme
  • 1 (11 ounce) package white chocolate chips
  • 3 teaspoons orange extract
  • 12 drops yellow food coloring
  • 9 drops red food coloring

  1. Grease a 9 x 13 inch pan.
  2. In a medium saucepan over medium heat, combine sugar, cream and butter. Heat to soft ball stage, 234 degrees F (112 degrees C). Remove from heat and stir in marshmallow creme and white chocolate chips; mix well until the chips melt. Reserve 1 cup of mixture and set aside.
  3. To the remaining mixture add orange flavoring, yellow and red food coloring. Stir well and pour into prepared pan. Pour reserved cream mixture on top. Using a knife, swirl layers for decorative effect.
  4. Chill for 2 hours, or until firm, and cut into squares.
